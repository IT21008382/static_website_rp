Empowering Minds with AI-Driven Insights

In today’s rapidly evolving educational landscape, EdSense, in collaboration with its sister companies Evently and Smart Sense, has undertaken a research initiative dedicated to harnessing AI to reshape classroom dynamics. Our study aims to enhance student engagement, learning efficiency, and teaching insights through innovative AI-driven systems. Evently’s contributions focus on real-time engagement monitoring, empowering lecturers with immediate feedback on students’ attentiveness and enabling adaptive teaching strategies. Meanwhile, Smart Sense enriches learning experiences through advanced video segmentation, question-based assessments, and gamified features designed to foster active participation. This multi-faceted approach promises to create a smarter, more responsive educational environment, supporting both learners and educators in achieving their best outcomes

LITERATURE SURVEY

Recent advancements in computer vision technologies, such as gaze tracking, body posture analysis, and facial expression detection, have paved the way for real-time classroom attention-monitoring systems that enhance student engagement and enable educators to individualize interventions. Researchers have successfully applied these technologies alongside machine learning models to assess attention levels in both physical and virtual classrooms, with high accuracy achieved through methods like gaze estimation and motion analysis. The integration of edge computing and high-performance models, including facial recognition, pose estimation, and gesture recognition technologies, promises a multi-model lecture monitoring system that efficiently captures student and lecturer interactions. By leveraging Jetson Nano devices with optimized algorithms, such as TensorRT-enhanced MobileNet V2, the system supports real-time processing for classroom applications. Additionally, analytical dashboards provide valuable feedback on teaching performance, promoting continuous improvement through actionable insights. This combination of technologies has the potential to create adaptive, data-driven educational environments.

The fusion of natural language processing (NLP) and machine learning (ML) in educational technology has enhanced student engagement and learning efficiency through adaptive, data-driven tools. These systems, as Gligorea et al. suggest, use ML algorithms to tailor content based on student performance and pace, while Kyoungwon et al. highlight the role of NLP-powered chatbots in facilitating real-time student-teacher interaction. AI-driven video segmentation further personalizes learning by dividing lectures into topic-based chapters, as shown by Dimitriadou et al., while dynamic quiz integration by Getenet et al. adapts question difficulty in real-time. Research into synchronous video tools, like Lingnau et al.'s Learnflix, reveals benefits for collaborative learning, although more studies are needed to confirm psychological outcomes. Khalil et al. found that synchronous communication enhances collaborative skills more effectively than asynchronous tools, fostering real-time engagement. Gamification, explored by Pechenkina et al., improves engagement and retention but requires further investigation across diverse student demographics to assess its long-term impact on motivation.

METHODOLOGY

This methodology integrates advanced computational and recognition technologies to optimize real-time performance within an educational video-based learning system. At the core, NVIDIA’s Jetson Nano and TensorRT technologies enable efficient model deployment, with TensorRT performing layer fusion, precision calibration, and kernel auto-tuning to reduce latency and boost throughput, which is ideal for edge computing environments. To detect the lecturer in real time, we employ the Mobilenet-SSD-v2 model trained on the COCO dataset, optimized via TensorRT. Using ONVIF protocols for PTZ (Pan-Tilt-Zoom) camera control, the system dynamically follows the lecturer based on detection data, continuously streaming the video via RTSP. Further enhancing the learning experience, the system leverages a custom-trained ResNet-18 model to recognize lecturer actions, which provides insights into engagement and presentation style. Integrated with facial recognition, this approach ensures accurate tracking of actions. For the content of the video, transcriptions are generated through OpenAI’s Whisper ASR model, which timestamps each word, enabling precise chapter segmentation. Text preprocessing techniques, such as tokenization, stopword removal, and lemmatization, ensure that only essential information is retained, while TextTiling identifies thematic shifts in the content. Google’s Flan-T5 model generates chapter titles, and the segments are stored in JSON format, allowing seamless integration with the video player. The system also incorporates a gamified Q&A module to strengthen learning engagement, using TF-IDF and RAKE to extract keywords for question generation via the T5 model. Contextual distractors are created with sense2vec, allowing students to test their understanding through multiple-choice questions triggered during playback. Real-time collaboration is achieved with a decoupled client-server structure where Node.js and Express handle synchronization, Redis manages session data, and MongoDB stores persistent data. Video synchronization and real-time communication through Video.js, Socket.io, and WebRTC allow students to interact dynamically via text and voice chat during the session. Finally, a recommendation system tailors content based on user feedback, processed through TF-IDF and a Multinomial Naive Bayes classifier. This classification enables the system to assess the perceived difficulty of the content, recommending videos that are either easier or more challenging based on user needs. Altogether, these elements create a responsive, interactive, and personalized learning environment that maximizes engagement and learning outcomes.

RESEARCH PROBLEM

Understanding whether a lecturer is conveying information comprehensively is crucial to fostering an engaging and effective learning environment. Often, even the most engaging lectures may fail to connect with students if key concepts aren’t communicated in a clear, digestible manner. To bridge this gap, AI-driven solutions can provide real-time feedback on student engagement and comprehension levels, helping lecturers identify when attention wanes or confusion arises. By monitoring signals such as eye movements, facial expressions, and interactive responses, these systems can alert lecturers to moments where students struggle, enabling on-the-fly adjustments to teaching strategies. Additionally, AI-based analysis of lecture patterns and student responses over time can provide insights into specific areas where content clarity or delivery may be lacking. Armed with this data, lecturers can make targeted improvements, crafting a more interactive and adaptive learning experience that not only retains attention but also drives deeper understanding. This approach not only enhances learning outcomes but also empowers lecturers to evolve their teaching methods based on actionable insights

To identify whether students are paying attention during a lecture in real-time, AI and sensor-based systems can monitor subtle cues that indicate engagement levels. Technologies like computer vision and machine learning analyze eye movements, body posture, and facial expressions, providing insights into students’ focus and attentiveness. For instance, gaze tracking can reveal if students are looking at the lecturer or materials, while posture analysis can identify signs of distraction or fatigue. Additionally, interactive feedback methods, such as real-time polling or quizzes, can engage students actively while providing the lecturer with data on comprehension and focus. When these systems detect a drop in attention levels, they can alert the lecturer, enabling immediate intervention—whether through a shift in teaching strategy, a break, or a brief interactive activity. This dynamic feedback loop empowers lecturers to keep students engaged throughout the session, creating a more responsive and effective learning environment.

The length of educational videos plays a critical role in student engagement and learning outcomes, as longer videos tend to test students' attention spans, often leading to cognitive overload and reduced retention of information. Studies suggest that shorter, segmented videos are more effective, allowing students to absorb content in manageable portions, which not only maintains focus but also enhances comprehension. Integrating Question-Based Assessments (QBAs) within these videos further amplifies engagement by actively involving students in the learning process. Videos with embedded questions encourage active recall, requiring students to process information and apply their understanding in real time. This interactive approach can significantly increase behavioral engagement, as students are more likely to remain attentive when they know assessments are embedded throughout the material. In contrast, lecture videos without QBAs often result in more passive learning, where students may drift or disengage over time. The combined approach of concise video length and interactive questioning has proven effective in fostering a more dynamic and engaged learning experience, ultimately leading to improved learning outcomes.

Combining gamification with asynchronous learning has transformative effects on student engagement and knowledge retention, shifting the learning environment from a teacher-centric to a student-centric model. Gamification elements—such as points, badges, and leaderboards—encourage students to participate actively, providing intrinsic motivation that drives them to engage with learning material in a sustained and meaningful way. In an asynchronous setting, students have the flexibility to interact with content at their own pace, allowing for a deeper focus on understanding and retention rather than rushing to meet rigid schedules. This approach fosters a collaborative learning model where students can work together on gamified tasks, share achievements, and compare progress, creating a sense of community even when learning remotely. When coupled with collaborative tools and novel technologies, gamified asynchronous learning environments can further enhance interactive experiences, allowing students to engage with both peers and the material actively and meaningfully. This model not only accommodates diverse learning styles but also leverages technology to create a dynamic, effective, and highly personalized educational experience.

RESEARCH GAP

Current research on classroom behavior recognition and attentiveness tracking presents valuable but limited insights. Existing studies primarily focus on singular or dual aspects of detection, such as face recognition, pose estimation, and gesture recognition. For instance, one study uses YOLO-v4 for sustainable behavior recognition in classrooms, while another leverages VGG16 for facial recognition with high accuracy. A separate study employs LeNet-5 for hand gesture classification, and another uses pose estimation with DNNs to track specific student behaviors. Additional studies examine student attentiveness through motion analysis and facial tracking, using tools like Kinect sensors and high-tech cameras, yet they yield only moderate accuracies. Although these methods provide insights into behaviors, they often lack multi-dimensional analysis, limiting their scope. A comprehensive system that integrates multiple behavioral and attentiveness dimensions could significantly improve the understanding of student engagement and teaching effectiveness, bridging existing research gaps. The research gap in AI-driven education highlights limitations in current systems' ability to integrate multiple AI functionalities to create a cohesive, adaptive learning environment. Current studies explore specific aspects like video segmentation, question generation, adaptive learning pathways, content recommendation, and adaptive assessments but treat these components as separate processes. For instance, video segmentation and question generation often lack dynamic interactivity, while adaptive learning systems primarily tailor course content without adjusting entire learning pathways. Similarly, content recommendation systems don’t fully account for complex learning goals, and adaptive assessments rarely consider the broader educational context. Collaborative tools, while useful, also fall short in seamless interaction and real-time feedback. There is an opportunity for a unified system that integrates video-based learning, interactive assessments, personalized recommendations, and collaborative features, offering a holistic, engaging, and personalized educational experience tailored to each student's needs and learning journey
